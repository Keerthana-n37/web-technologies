About Nature

Nature includes forests, oceans, mountains, rivers, animals, and plants. It provides essential resources such as oxygen, water, and food. Human life depends completely on nature for survival.

However, due to pollution and deforestation, the environment is under threat. Protecting natural ecosystems is necessary to maintain balance on Earth.

“The Earth is what we all have in common.”

Organizations like WWF and UNEP work worldwide to protect the environment.

Wildlife Conservation

Wildlife refers to animals and plants that live in natural habitats. Many species are becoming endangered due to habitat loss and illegal hunting. Conservation programs help protect biodiversity.

Important Wildlife Animals

  • Tiger
  • Elephant
  • Panda
  • Snow Leopard
  • Giant Tortoise

Wildlife sanctuaries and national parks help protect animals from extinction.

Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature and weather patterns. Human activities like burning fossil fuels increase greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

Effects of Climate Change

  1. Rising global temperatures
  2. Melting glaciers
  3. Rising sea levels
  4. Extreme weather conditions
  5. Loss of biodiversity

Steps to reduce climate change: 

1. Use renewable energy
2. Reduce plastic usage
3. Plant more trees
4. Save electricity
5. Promote recycling
